“A high return on investment can still be a poor decision.”

My final-year project on outdoor advertising profitability in Casablanca began as a financial problem: compare locations, costs, revenues, and ROI. The deeper lesson was managerial. Traffic volume alone is insufficient. A defensible investment decision must also account for regulation, operating costs, location quality, socio-economic context, data reliability, maintenance risk, and implementation feasibility.

That insight now shapes my MBA research direction: transformation projects should be assessed through strategy, finance, operations, governance, trust, and ethical reasoning together.

MBA Research Proposal

An Integrated MBA Readiness Framework for Assessing AI-Enabled Digital Transformation in Saudi Smart-City Megaprojects: A NEOM Case Study

Managerial readiness, not technical hype.

The project develops an MBA-level managerial assessment framework. It is not a technical AI system, not a confidential audit of NEOM, and not a claim about real implementation outcomes. NEOM is used as a bounded conceptual case setting. The research avoids confidential NEOM data and compares a generic digital-transformation checklist with an integrated readiness model.
