Proposed Research

A Discounted Total Cost of Ownership and BWM-TOPSIS Framework for Value-Based Procurement of High-Cost Medical Devices in Saudi Public Hospitals

The proposal develops a reproducible, non-clinical decision-support model for procurement committees. It combines lifecycle affordability with operational, supplier, governance, digital-readiness, and sustainability criteria.

The lowest acquisition price is not always the lowest-cost decision.

My previous work in financial modelling, portfolio analysis, and procurement-related decision support repeatedly showed that headline cost can hide long-term exposure. Maintenance, delays, operational constraints, supplier risk, training, and replacement requirements can change the value of an investment after the initial decision.

This insight now guides my proposed research in hospital management: high-cost medical devices should be compared through transparent lifecycle cost and non-price criteria, without making clinical claims or relying on confidential supplier data.

Decision Model

From purchase price to defensible lifecycle value

Discounted Total Cost of Ownership makes the full financial commitment visible. The Best-Worst Method structures criteria weighting informed by specialist judgment, TOPSIS ranks alternatives by closeness to an ideal value-based option, and sensitivity analysis tests whether the result remains stable when assumptions change.
